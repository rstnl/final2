Write This Site

From the the Internet Archive to the Wayback Machine, we're all familiar with the perceived permanence of the web. A "digital footprint". Posts made with the understanding that they may never truly be erased.

This is not quite the case.

Eventually, almost everything is lost to time, especially in a space as vast as the internet.

This project focuses on the ways humans experience time and how that affects our memories and record-keeping. Like any medium, there are limitations to what can be maintained over an extended period of time. A book can only have so many pages, a song can only have so many lyrics, and a person can only have so many memories. The internet, as wide and endless as it may seem, is only an extension of memories, and thus, has a limit of its own.

This work itself is original as it focuses on the ways humans experience time and how that affects our memories and record-keeping. A book can only have so many pages, a song can only have so many lyrics, and a person can only have so many memories. The internet, as wide and endless as it may seem, is only an extension of memories, and thus, has a limit of its own.

"Write This Site" is an almost-empty page with an input box repeatedly falling and re-entering the screen, giving the user a chance to add text. This acts as a space for the user to use the page to say something of their own. Despite the belief that everything posted online will last forever, the text will fade. The project encourages users to leave a message, one they know will not stand the test of time, for the sole act of participating. The artistic merits of this project lies in its interactivity, as people are more likely to remember an artwork if they are an active participant. It also develops the idea that art is shaped by one's experiences, which are, of course, shaped by those around us. In no way is any one artist not influenced by their peers, so that is something that is reflected in my project.

One day, this project will cease to exist. Whether it be through the link becoming defunct, a complete purge of the internet, or simply it being viewed for the last time before being forgotten, "Write This Site" will be no more.

Enjoy your stay!
