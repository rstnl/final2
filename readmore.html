Write This Site

An almost-empty page with an input box in the middle that gives the user a chance to add any text. Upon clicking or hitting enter, the text disappears. This acts as a space for the user to use the page to say something of their own. Despite the belief that everything posted online will last forever, the text will fade. Eventually, almost everything is lost to time, especially in a space as vast as the internet.

The artistic merits of this project lie in its interactivity, as people are more likely to remember an artwork if they are an active participant. It also develops the idea that art is shaped by one's experiences, which are, of course, shaped by those around us. In no way is any one artist not influenced by their peers, so that is something that is reflected in my project.

This work itself is original as it focuses on the ways humans experience time and how that affects our memories and record-keeping. Like any medium, there are limitations what can be maintained over an extended period of time. A book can only have so many pages, a song can only have so many lyrics, and a person can only have so many memories. The internet, as wide and endless as it may seem, is only an extension of memories, and thus, has a limit of its own.

This project encourages users to leave a message, one they know will not stand the test of time, for the sole act of participating.
